Privacy Policy

What data we collect

When you connect your Google account, we store:

  • OAuth tokens (access and refresh) — used to interact with YouTube and Google Drive on your behalf
  • Your channel name — displayed in the settings page
  • Upload job metadata — video title, description, privacy status, playlist assignment, and upload timestamps
  • Google Drive folder reference — the folder ID you configure for automatic polling
How tokens are stored

All OAuth tokens are encrypted at rest using AES encryption before being persisted to the database. They are never logged or exposed in the UI.

What we access

Depending on your chosen permission tier:

  • Basic: upload videos to YouTube, read your channel info and playlists, list and download files from your configured Google Drive folder
  • Extended: everything above, plus adding uploaded videos to a playlist and removing files from Google Drive after upload

We never access Drive files outside your configured folder. We never modify or delete your existing YouTube videos.

Video content

Videos are streamed directly from Google Drive to YouTube through the server's memory. They are never written to disk or retained after the upload completes.

Data retention

Upload job metadata (title, status, timestamps) is kept until you delete it from the queue. OAuth tokens are kept until you disconnect your account or delete your data.

Third parties

No data is shared with third parties. The application communicates only with Google APIs (YouTube Data API and Google Drive API) on your behalf.

Data deletion

You can disconnect your YouTube account at any time from the Settings page, which removes all stored OAuth tokens and your Drive folder configuration.

Open source

This application is fully open source. You can audit the code or self-host your own instance.
